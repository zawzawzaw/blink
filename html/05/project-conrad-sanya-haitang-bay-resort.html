Conrad Sanya Haitang Bay

Haitang Bay is situated approximately 28 kilometers northeast of Sanya, an international leisure and resort area and an important location for conventions.

The Haitang Bay coastline is renowned for its natural beauty, miles of soft white-sand beaches, watersports, landmarks, temples and statues. While minimalist contemporary was the prevailing style trend in the marketplace at the time, the designers of BLINK sought to reinterpret the concept of luxury in China and create a completely new resort that would draw from classical themes and embrace a modern sensibility.

Inspiration was drawn from French classical architecture found in Marseille, which was recently designated as the European Capital of Culture. BLINK’s design incorporates a carefully calibrated level of opulence with nature to strike a chord with discriminating travelers seeking utter luxury and seclusion in a tropical paradise and also resonated well with the Chinese market’s perceptions of luxury.

The design of the resort is simple yet elegant, featuring long lines, stone–arched walkways and fluted columns. Sweeping courtyards allow the outdoors to be brought into almost every room. Water features are placed throughout the gardens to soften hard edges and add to the visitor’s delight with shimmering mirages of the natural and built surroundings.

The Conrad Sanya Haitang Bay set a new standard in China and was named “Best Luxury Hotel in the World” at the prestigious annual Hospitality Design Awards. This had also garnered for the property extensive press coverage.
